## In a Nutshell

<img style="float: right; width: 42%; padding: 10px;" src="img/portrait.png">

<p align="left"> Hi, I'm Ben. I build machine-learning models inspired by biology and physics to understand how intelligence and artificial life emerge in physical systems.</p>

<p align="left"> My work sits at the intersection of soft and active matter physics, non-equilibrium thermodynamics, complex biological systems, and collective multi-agent learning. I’m interested in how computation, adaptation, and cognition arise and integrate in composite dynamical systems. </p>

<p align="left"> During my studies at TU Wien, I worked across disciplines — from 
  <a class="" target='blank' href="https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.115.033601">quantum optics</a> and 
  <a class="" target='blank' href="https://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/acs.jctc.9b01251">computational materials science</a> to 
  <a class="" target='blank' href="https://www.pnas.org/content/118/19/e2019683118">reinforcement learning models of cellular decision-making</a>. 
After my PhD, I collaborated with industrial partners on biologically inspired, robust autonomous navigation in maritime environments.</p>

<p align="left"> My current resarch in the <a class="" target='blank' href="https://drmichaellevin.org/">Levin Lab</a> at the Allen Discovery Center at Tufts University explores the <a class="" target='blank' href="https://doi.org/10.3390/e26070532">Evolutionary Implications of Self-Assembling Cybernetic Materials with Collective Problem-Solving Intelligence at Multiple Scales</a>. I develop generative multi-agent-based machine learning techniques to model and train <a class="" target='blank' href="https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/systems-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnsys.2022.768201/full">multi-scale competency architectures that form the foundation of biological organization</a>: swarms of virtual, adaptive, communicating agents that implement a minimal model for evolutionary morphogenesis of multi-cellular tissue based on individual decision-making.</p>

<p align="left"> More broadly, I study how developmental biology, biophysics, and artificial life methods — including 
  <a class="" target='blank' href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.plrev.2025.11.010">neural cellular automata</a>
  and neuroevolution — can be combined to design decentralized decision-making policies. These approaches enable <a class="" target='blank' href="https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2407.09438">robust autonomous navigation in virtual microswimmers</a> and open new paths toward physiological computation in soft-matter systems.</p>

<p align="left"> My goal is to understand and reframe learning, cognition, and biological & artificial intelligence as a collective, scale-free phenomenon — revealing new perspectives on fundamental biological processes such as evolution, computation, intelligence, and life at the edge of order and chaos.
</p>

<p align="left"> I’m a curious, creative interdisciplinary scientist with a deep enthusiasm for AI, technology, physics, and (artificial) life. </p>
